Profile

I'm an Electrical Engineering student who found a stronger pull toward data and decision-making than toward circuits — and rebuilt my skill set around it. Over the past year I've worked through six independent, self-directed projects that each start the way a business analyst's work starts: with a stakeholder question, not a dataset.

My approach carries over directly from engineering — define the problem precisely, test assumptions, and don't present a conclusion you can't defend. What's changed is the output: instead of a circuit diagram, I now deliver a recommendation, a risk score, or a report a non-technical stakeholder can act on.
